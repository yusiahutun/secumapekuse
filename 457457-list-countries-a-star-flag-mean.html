A flag is a symbol of national unity in every country. It is treated with respect as it has significance to its people. Flags are interesting to study as each flag for every independent nation has a story. Standard designs in them include stripes and stars. There are dozens of countries with a star on their flag.

How many countries have a star on their flag? Multiple countries have stars on their flags, each symbolizing something distinct. The star is as important as any design of the flag. Therefore, flags are seen as significant and handled with respect. In some countries, it is considered treasonous if you disrespect or even burn your country's flag.

Countries with a star on their flag

How many countries have a star on their flag? More than 30 countries have a star on their flags. Here is a list of these countries and what these symbols mean.

1. Algeria

The Algerian flag is a vertical bicolour of green and white with the red crescent encircling the red five-pointed star centred along the dividing line. The white on the flag represents peace, while the green, star, and crescent represent Islam.

Red is a crucial colour on the Algerian flag as it represents the bloodshed during the fight for independence. The flag was adopted on 3rd July 1962.

2. Aruba

Aruba is one of four countries in the Kingdom of the Netherlands. It is a constituent country with a population of 107000 people. The flag was adopted on 18th March 1976 and had four colours.

The flag has a field of light blue and two narrow parallel horizontal yellow stripes in the bottom half. It also has a four-pointed white-fimbriated red star. The red star symbolizes the love of the country, and the white outline surrounding represents the white sandy beaches around Aruba.

3. Azerbaijan

Azerbaijan is among the six modern independent Turkic states with a majority Muslim population. The flag consists of a horizontal tricolour of bright blue, red, and green, with a white crescent and an eight-pointed star placed on the red line.

The colours on the flag have meaning; the red colour symbolizes progress, green Islam, and blue represents Azerbaijan's Turkic heritage. The flag was adopted on 9th November 1918.

4. Burkina Faso

The West African nation is among the countries with recognizable stars on its flag. Burkina Faso's flag has red and green colours, and a yellow five-pointed star is centred on the flag.

The green band on the flag represents how agriculturally rich the country is, while the red embodies blood shed during the revolution. The yellow star is a sign of a democratic popular revolution. The former French colony adopted her flag on 4th August 1984.

5. Cameroon

The Republic of Cameroon is in west-central Africa. Its flag has three colours; green, red and yellow bands running vertically. There is also a five-pointed gold star on the red band.

The red represents unity, the yellow sun, and also the savannas in northern Cameroon, and the yellow forests in the southern part of the country. The gold star is referred to as the star of unity. Cameroonians adopted the flag on 20th May 1975.

6. The Central African Republic

The central African republic flag was adopted on 1st December 1958. The flag is multi-coloured and one of the most appealing flags In Africa. In the centre, African Republic has four horizontal stripes of blue, white, green and yellow intersected by one vertical stripe of red in the middle, with a yellow five-pointed star in the canton.

The blue on the flag symbolizes liberty, grandeur, and the sky, while white is for purity, equality, and candour. The colour green stands for the nation's forests, while yellow is for. Finally, red represents the common blood of all humankind.

7. Chile

Chile is located in South America. The Chilean flag consists of a horizontal bicolour of white and red with the blue square-ended on the upper hoist-side corner of the white band bearing the white five-pointed star in the middle.

The Chilean flag was adopted on 18th October 1817. The star on it represents a guide to honour and progress, while the blue, white, and red colours stand for the sky and the Pacific, the snow-covered Andes, and the blood spilt during the fight for independence respectively.

8. Congo Kinshasa

The Democratic Republic of the Congo is also known as Congo-Kinshasa, DR Congo, or Congo-Zaire. The central African country's national flag has a sky blue field, decorated with a yellow star in the upper left canton and cut diagonally by a red stripe with a yellow fimbriation.

The blue stands for peace, the red represents the country's martyrs and the yellow wealth of the nation. The star on the flag symbolizes the country's bright future.

9. Cuba

The republic of Cuba comprises the island of Cuba and Isla de la Juventud, and several minor archipelagos. The flag has five horizontal stripes of blue alternate with white with the red equilateral triangle based on the hoist-side bearing the white five-pointed star in the centre.

The three blue stripes represent the departments in which the island was then divided. The two white stripes stand for equality, liberty, and fraternity. Cubans adopted the flag on 20th May 1902.

10. Cyprus Northern

Northern Cyprus is a territory of the Republic of Cyprus under Turkish occupation. However, the Republic of Turkey recognizes Nothern Cyprus as a sovereign state. The Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus flag is based on the flag of Turkey, with the colours reversed and two additional horizontal red stripes at the top and bottom.

The flag has two colours; red and white. The red star and crescent stand for Turkishness, red representing the blood of Turkish Cypriots killed in Cypriot intercommunal chaos.

11. Djibouti

Djibouti is a landlocked country on the horn of Africa. Its flag was adopted on 27th June 1977 and symbolises pride to Djiboutians. The flag has three colours and a single five-pointed red star. The colours on the Djibouti flag denote the following; light blue, the sky, green represents the endless green of the Earth, and the red star stands for unity and the bloodshed of martyrs.

12. Ethiopia

The Ethiopian flag consists of a green, yellow, and red tricolour with the National Emblem, a golden pentagram/star on a blue disc, superimposed at the centre.

The star represents the unity of all Ethiopian nationalities and rays the bright prospects for their future. Blue symbolizes peace, yellow hope, justice, and equality. Red is for offering freedom and equality, while green is equated to labour and fertility.

13. Ghana

The Ghanaian flag consists of a horizontal triband of red, gold, and green, charged with a black star in the centre. The red represents the blood spilt during the fight for independence, the yellow represents the country's mineral wealth, and the green symbolizes Ghana's vegetation and natural wealth.

14. Israel

Israel is a Middle Eastern country on the Mediterranean Sea. The government adopted its current double-coloured flag on 28th October 1948.

The flag has the colours blue and white on the Israeli flag symbolizes the stripes on a tallit, the traditional Jewish prayer shawl. The star represents David, a symbol broadly recognized by the Jewish people and Judaism.

15. Jordan

The Jordanian flag comprises a horizontal triband of black, white and green, with a red chevron based on the hoist side containing a white seven-pointed star. The chevron is to honour the Hashemite dynasty and the Arab Revolt.

The seven-pointed star is a symbol of unity and the seven verses of Al-Fatiha. The flag was adopted on 16th April 1928

16. Liberia

Liberia's flag is eerily similar to the U.S flag, the only difference being the number of stripes and stars. The flag's colours are red, white and blue, with the star being on a blue field at the top left while the rest of the flag has red and white stripes.

17. Malaysia

The Malaysian flag consists of fourteen horizontal stripes alternating red and white, a yellow crescent and a fourteen-point star on a blue field in the canton. The flag was adopted on 26th May 1960.

The red, white and blue colours signify that the south-east Asian country belongs in the Commonwealth. The Crescent moon and star represent Islam, the official religion in Malaysia.

18. Morocco

The north African nation is one of the most well-liked countries with flags with one star. The Moroccan flag has a red field with a green pentagram/star. The flag was adopted on 17th November 1915. The red colour symbolizes the ancestors' blood and unity, while the green star stands for the five pillars of Islam.

19. North Korea

The Democratic People's Republic of Korea is located in East Asia. Its flag consists of a large red stripe at the centre, enclosed by a narrow white stripe above and below. It also has blue stripes above and below.

The five-pointed red star is on the red stripe. The flag is symbolic, with the red star representing communism, red panels embodying patriotism, the white stripe representing unification and culture, and the blue stripe representing unity. The flag was adopted on 10th July 1948.

20. Pakistan

In South Asia, Pakistan is one of the many Muslim countries whose flags have the crescent moon and a single star. The Pakistani flag has a green field with a vertical white stripe at the hoist and white crescent moon, and a white five-pointed star at the centre.

The green and white symbolize peace and prosperity. The crescent represents progress, and the star represents light and knowledge. The white stripe was added in 1947 to honour minority groups. Pakistanis adopted the flag on 11th August 1947.

21. Puerto Rico

Puerto Rico is a Caribbean island, and the unincorporated U.S. Puerto Rico flag shares its colours with the American flag but has a different design. The white star represented the island of Puerto Rico.

The three red stripes are to pay homage to the brave warriors, while the white stripes symbolize the presence of victory and peace after independence. The blue represents the sky and blue coastal waters. The Puerto Rico flag was adopted in 1952.

22. Senegal

The Senegalese flag was adopted on 20th August 1960. It consists of three vertical green, yellow and red bands charged with a five-pointed green star at the centre. The colour green symbolizes hope, while yellow is for natural riches and the wealth derived from the citizens' work. Red is a symbol of the independence struggle, life, and socialism.

23. Somalia

Somalia is another country that has a simple flag; a blue field with a five-pointed white star in the middle. The United Nations flag inspired the blue field and symbolizes the surrounding Indian Ocean and the blue sky above.

24. Suriname

The South American country adopted its flag on 25th November 1975. The flag consists of a horizontal triband of green at the top and bottom, red with a large white border and a large yellow five-pointed star centred on the red band.

The colours red stand for progress, white for freedom and justice, and green for fertile lands. The yellow star is symbolic of unity and a golden future.

25. Togo

The Togolese flag was adopted on 28th April 1960. It consists of five equal horizontal bands of green alternating with yellow and a white five-pointed star on a red square on the upper hoist-side.

Like every other country, the colours and stars on the flag of Togo are symbolic. Red is for bloodshed by Martyrs to gain independence, the White star symbolizes hope, green is for the vegetation and nature, and yellow represents the natural resources in Togo.

26. Tunisia

Tunisia is a north African country with about 12 million people. The country's flag consists of a white sun disc containing a red five-pointed star surrounded by a red crescent laying on a red field. The crescent and star are perceived as traditional symbols of Islam and regarded as good luck symbols. The flag was adopted in 1831

27. Turkey

Turkey is another of the many countries whose flag has a crescent moon and star. The national flag of Turkey consists of a white crescent moon and star over a red field. The moon and star are insignia for Turkic peoples. The red field signifies blood shed by Turkey warriors. The national flag of Turkey was adopted in 1844.

28. Vietnam

What flag has a single star? Vietnam is one among the many countries with single stars. The South-East Asian country has one of the simplest flags; a five-pointed yellow star over a red field.

The red in the Vietnamese flag symbolizes the blood spilt during the revolution. The golden star is to pay homage to the five main classes in Vietnamese society â€” intellectuals, farmers, workers, entrepreneurs, and soldiers. The flag was adopted on 5th September 1945.

29. Western Sahara

Western Sahara is a disputed territory on the northwest coast and in the Maghreb region of North and West Africa. Its flag has a black, white and green horizontal tricolour charged with a red star and crescent in the centre stripe and a red chevron at the hoist.

The flag's colours are symbolic, with the black line representing death, the white stripe peace, and the green strip symbolises life. The red triangle on the flag stands for progressive politics and the state's revolutionary heritage.

30. Zimbabwe

The flag of Zimbabwe has seven even horizontal stripes of green, gold, red and black with a white triangle containing a red 5-pointed star and a Zimbabwe Bird. The colour green symbolizes the country's vegetation and land resources, yellow mineral wealth, and red blood lost during the liberation period. The southern African country adopted its current flag on 18th April 1980
