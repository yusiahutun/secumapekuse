Vivian Salama’s Age and Career

Vivian Salama was born on July 1, 1980, in New York City, the United States. As of 2019, she is 39 years old, and her birth sign is cancer. Her nationality is American and is of mixed ethnicity. Her mother Charlotte Georges Farag is a native of Cairo, Egypt. She has three siblings; two sisters named Venise Fayez and Rosette Attia and a brother named Ragui Marcos.

Regarding her academics, Salama earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in Journalism and Theater at Rutgers University. In 2004, she attended American University in Cairo.

Similarly, she received an MA degree in Islamic Politics from Columbia University in the City of New York. Also,  Salama graduated from Georgetown University Law Center from where she gained a Doctor of Law.

Besides, Salama started her career as a producer in WNBC. After working in the network for almost four years, she joined Newsweek Magazine, USA Today, International Herald Tribune, and Voice of America as a freelance contributor. Similarly, from 2006 to 2008, she served as a producer in ABC News.

Moreover, Vivian also served at several networks including Rutgers University as an adjunct lecturer, TIME.com as a freelance journalist, and as a correspondent at Bloomberg LP. From January 2017 to September 2017 she worked at The Associated Press as an Iraq Bureau Chief, Deputy U.S. Political Editor, Foreign Policy Reporter, and White House Reporter.

After leaving the network, she joined NBC News as a political Reporter. Since 2018, she is serving as a White House Reporter in The Wall Street Journal.
