An American actress, director, spokesperson, and activist who is best known for starring in Nip/Tuck and One Tree Hill. She is daughter to Maureen and Charles William Bush. In 2000 she completed her graduation from Westridge School for Girls. She has done major in journalism from University of Southern California. She debuted in film industry in 2002 film National Lampoon's Van Wilder. She has also made guest appearance in several TV series like Nip/Tuck, Sabrina, Point of Origin and the Teenage Witch. Beside acting she is also spokeswoman of Dosomething.org. She has been awarded with Choice Movie Breakout – Female by Teen Choice Awards in 2007 for her role in movie The Hitcher and John Tucker Must Die.

Early Life and Education of Sophia Bush

Sophia Bush was born on July 8, 1982, in Pasadena, California to Maureen Bush and Charles William Bush. She graduated from  Westridge School for Girls in 2000 where she was in a volleyball team. At the age of 17, she won the Tournament of Roses Parade Queen.

Later, Bush attended the University of Southern California as a journalism major, she was also a member and  Social Chair of the Kappa Kappa Gamma sorority.

Sophia Bush's Career

Sophia Bush started her professional acting career in 2002 in the film, National Lampoon's Van Wilder opposite Ryan Reynolds. Then she appeared in a number of TV series including  Nip/Tuck and Sabrina the Teenage Witch.

In 2003, Bush starred in the WB television series, One Tree Hill as Brooke Davis along with James Lafferty, Hilarie Burton, Bethany Joy Len, and others. Some of her notable screen credit includes, The Hitcher, The Narrows, Chalet Girl, Partners, Chicago Fire, Stay Alive and others. 

AS of 2018, Bush provided the voice of Voyd in Pixar's film, Incredibles 2.
