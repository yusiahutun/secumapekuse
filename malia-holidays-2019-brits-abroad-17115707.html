Malias WILD party pics revealed: The sexiest summer ever?

 Posted on July 5, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  378 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Our fellow countrymen and women enjoyed frolics and steamy parties in the Greek island of Crete.

Malia is the place to be for music lovers with some of the best events of this kind in Europe including Malia Live at Candy Club and Go Deep at Apollo.

Then of course the Malia Booze Cruise takes the banging nightlife off shore and onto the water.

Daily Star Online witnessed raucous scenes on the Malia strip last summer.

Revellers danced on podiums, got into fights and boozed into the early hours of the morning.

The Greek resort has an incredible nightlife with four world famous clubs within a stone's throw of each other.

From passed-out naked lads and pole dancing to paint parties and horrific drunken tattoos, Malia has it all.

Instagram was awash with jubilant partygoers enjoying the Greek hotspot, uploading their favourite snaps from their weeks of fun in the sun.

Some gobsmacking tattoos were on show, a lad partied with a pink traffic cone on his head, while another lad shared an image of his mate passed out naked in a bath tub.

Hot lasses also shared their party pics on social media.

They were having such a wild time that tourism chiefs in the area spoke out against the Brits abroad.

While warnings were issued by the Foreign Office.

The former deputy mayor of Malia, now the deputy mayor of Hersonissos tourism and employment, Efthymios Moutrakis, responded directly to a Daily Star Online article on local radio last summer.

He criticised the suggestion the town was a destination for fun without limits.

Speaking to Heraklion radio station SKAI Kritis 92.1 Moudrakis said that over the past eight years, especially in the last two years, Malia has become a family tourism destination.

"If someone walks in Malia they will come across such images," he said referring to photos of Brit revellers enjoying the nightlife.

But he added "this image does not express the region as a whole”.

Moudrakis claimed that young Brits only represented a tiny number of the total amount of tourists to the area in the north of Crete.

He added: “Over the past 10 years, more than 10 clubs have closed in Malia, and facilities targeted to the target group have been upgraded.”
