Whitney Houston's brother Gary Houston performs at inaugural gala for the late singer's foundation

 Posted on October 9, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  664 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Whitney Houston’s brother Gary Houston took the stage Tuesday in Atlanta at an inaugural gala for The Whitney E. Houston Legacy Foundation.

Houston performed in front of a backdrop that showed he and his late sister singing at the event, on what would have been the late singer's 59th birthday,

The event drew a number of notable names to The Biltmore Ballrooms, including actor Chris Tucker and singer CeCe Winans, as well as relatives of the famed late singer, such as Gary and his wife Pat Houston. 

The latest: Whitney Houston’s brother Gary Houston, 64, took the stage Tuesday in Atlanta on what would have been the late singer's 59th birthday, at an inaugural gala for The Whitney E. Houston Legacy Foundation

The foundation was founded in 2020 as 'a continuation of Whitney's journey to rebuild lives, restore self esteem and repair images through grassroots programs and initiatives,' according to a statement on its website. 

The charity's mission statement 'is to inspire the youth, create opportunities and empower them to succeed beyond their expectations, creating a lasting solution to their challenges with hopes of improving their lives in a positive way.'

Others in attendance at the event included Whitney's niece Rayah Victoria Houston, real estate professional Chinye Enurah and newscaster Karyn Greer.

The famed singer, who had hits such as I Will Always Love You, How Will I Know and I Wanna Dance with Somebody (Who Loves Me), tragically died February 11, 2012 when she accidentally drowned in a bathtub at the Beverly Hilton.

Compounding the tragedy, Houston's daughter Bobbi Kristina Brown died at the age of 22 in July of 2015, due to 'immersion associated with drug intoxication,' according to medical examiners in Atlanta. Brown had been found unresponsive in a bathtub six months prior to her passing.
