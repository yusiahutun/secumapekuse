Never Jump in Leaf Piles

 Posted on May 26, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  405 words  |  Larita Shotwell

From the crisp sound as you walk on them to the beautiful colors, autumn leaves are one of the best parts of the season. For kids, the best part is, of course, jumping in a pile of them. But before you let them leap, you might want to keep some of these hidden dangers in mind.

If the leaves are wet, for instance, there's a risk of coming in contact with harmful bacteria, like mold. Mold exposure can be especially dangerous for anyone with a mold allergy. If the child also has asthma, the problem could be exacerbated. In those cases, Michael Beninger, MD tells Weather Channel that "avoidance is your best strategy." (For parents with mold allergies who have to rake their own leaves, wear a respirator mask.)

While unlikely, that fun-looking pile could also be home to parasites like ticks. "If you rake a bunch of leaves up from [where your yard meets the woods]… there is a possibility that there is a tick but only if there are ticks there to begin with," pest expert Jim Dill told Bangor Daily News.

Leaf piles could also scratch or injure your child if rocks or sticks have collected in them. For piles that have been sitting for a few days, bugs, frogs, and even snakes could be creeping there. That's why Sarah A. Denny, MD, a pediatrician at Nationwide Children's Hospital in Columbus, Ohio, told Safe Bee that leaves should be disposed of as fast as possible after raking.

Getty Images

An outcome far more dangerous than those already mentioned could occur if your child goes after piles specifically meant for leaf pick-up, as there have been instances of children hiding or playing in the piles being run over by cars. In a 2014 incident, for example, two young girls lost their lives because the driver didn't see them in the pile.

But it's worth noting that the above risks are minimal if the leaves are freshly raked and are in a safe place—though, for the sake of your child's health and safety, it's probably best they leave the leaves alone.
