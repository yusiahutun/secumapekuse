Leeds United owner defends club's targeting of Karen Carney in Twitter rant over promotion

 Posted on September 15, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  477 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Leeds United owner Andrea Radrizzani has taken full responsibility for the club’s controversial tweet in response to Karen Carney, insisting the pundit’s criticism of Marcelo Bielsa’s side was “completely unnecessary and disrespectful”.

Carney, a former England Women’s international, suggested on Amazon Prime Sport that Leeds would have failed to gain promotion to the Premier League last season if football wasn’t put on hold for three months due to the coronavirus pandemic.

The ex-Lionesses star believes Bielsa’s men would have run out of steam if it wasn’t for the break in schedule, which sparked controversy on social media.

“[Leeds] out-run everyone and credit to them,” Carney said on Amazon Prime before their 5-0 win over West Brom on Tuesday night.

“My only concern would be, ‘Would they blow up at the end of the season?’

“We saw that in the last couple of seasons and I actually think they got promoted because of Covid in terms of it gave them a bit of respite.

“I don't know whether they would have gone up if they didn't have that break.”

In response to Carney’s comments, which ex-United striker Jimmy Floyd Hasselbaink agreed with on air, Leeds poked fun at the pundit on their official Twitter account by writing alongside a set of emojis: “‘Promoted because of Covid’ - Won the league by 10 points - Hi @primevideosport”.

The Yorkshire club have now come in for criticism of their own over the post, with many blasting them for exposing Carney to abuse on social media.

However, Radrizzani has taken fully responsibility for the tweet and insists the pundit’s comments were offensive to his players and coaches.
