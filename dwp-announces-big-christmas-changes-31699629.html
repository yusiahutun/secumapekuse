DWP announces big Christmas changes it could impact Universal Credit claimants

 Posted on July 8, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  463 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Christmas is right around the corner.

It means a lot of companies will begin to unwind ahead of the festive period. Now the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) announced some changes for next week.

The news could impact Universal Credit claimants and those on other benefits. It will take place around the bank holidays over the season.

All Jobcentre Plus outlets will shut on the following days:

  • Monday, December 25, 2023
  • Tuesday, December 26, 2023
  • Wednesday, December 27, 2023 (Jobcentres in Scotland will be open as normal)
  • Monday, January 1, 2024

Phone lines will also be closed on the days above. But phone lines are open on December 22 and January 22, operating on reduced hours.

The lines will close at 4pm instead of 6pm. There are 639 Jobcentres across the country and you can locate your closest one by typing in your postcode on the government's website.

Jobcentres are there to assist those on Universal Credit to find and get back into work. When you apply for Universal Credit, you'll need to visit your local Jobcentre where you'll be assigned a work coach and centre case manager.

All the advice and help provided is free of charge and the work coach helps customers look for work that is most suited to their skills.

It comes as the exact date DWP will pay Universal Credit claimants ahead of Christmas was revealed. This year, Christmas Day (December 25) falls on a Monday, while Boxing Day (December 26) is on Tuesday.

These days aren't classed as working days. And the DWP doesn't usually make benefit payments on bank holidays. So if you're due cash on these days, you'll likely be paid earlier this month.

You could be affected if you claim any of the following benefits:

  • Attendance allowance
  • Carer's allowance
  • Child benefit
  • Disability living allowance
  • Income support
  • Jobseekers allowance
  • Pension Credit
  • Personal independence payment (PIP)
  • State Pension
  • Tax credits
  • Universal Credit

The amount you get paid should not change. It will be the same as the previous month unless you've had a change of circumstances.
