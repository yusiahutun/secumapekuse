Police Officer Who Silenced Gaza Gang Asks Thika Thugs To Return Police Rifle Before He Goes For It

 Posted on March 9, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  528 words  |  Larita Shotwell
  • Criminals fear Ali Nuno due to his no-mercy approach to Gaza Gang members who attempted to terrorise residents of Kayole when he served as the OCPD
  • He is the current county commander of police in Kiambu county, having served in Nyeri in the same capacity
  • The police officer told criminals who had robbed a police officer of a gun to return it, or he would go for it and let them pay for the consequences

Michael Ollinga is a journalist at TUKO.co.ke with over 10 years of experience covering courts and crimes, special reports, and current affairs in Kenya.

A dreaded police officer known for cracking down on criminal gangs has issued a stern warning to thugs who robbed an officer of a gun.

Who is Ali Nuno?

The officer is the current Kiambu Police Commander, Ali Nuno, who was transferred from Nyeri in 2019.

Nuno is remembered for neutralising criminal gangs like Gaza and Mungiki, which were terrorising the residents of Nairobi Eastlands when he served as the OCPD Kayole.

It was during his reign as Kayole OCPD that anonymous criminal busters using the pseudonyms Hessy Wa Kayole, Hessy Wa Dandora, and Saigon neutralised members of the then-feared Gaza gang, pushing them from the city to the neighbouring Kiambu area.

Meet Kiambu county commander of police

This was back in 2015, after which he was promoted and assigned to the county commander position.

About 10 years later, Nuno has lost not a chip in his sternness when combating crime.

Speaking during a recent public event, Nuno warned thugs terrorising Thika individuals from their dens in Kiandutu that they would be dealt with more force than they could handle.

He was categorical, saying that a group of thugs that had robbed a police officer of his gun had two options: either return the firearm or wait for him to go for it.

Nuno warned that, if the thugs chose the latter, they would bear the consequences, which would not exclude a loss of lives.

Feared police officer's message to Kiandutu criminals

"Mrudishe hizo bunduki, na msiporudisha Return those guns or else) I will come for them. The issue is that when I come for them, there will be a cost to be paid. Even if it means losing lives, I won’t mind.I want the guns back, all of them. And if you don’t return, remember that I am not a new man in this game; I was the OCPD Kayole, wapi Gaza? (Where is Gaza?). I am not new in this game; just return the guns, or I will come for them. All guns in the wrong hands must be returned. If we don’t get them today, we should get them as early as yesterday. I hope you hear me people of Kiandutu,"Nuno said.
