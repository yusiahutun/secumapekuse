Jake Brennan Strikes A Deal With iHeartMedia For Music-based True Crime Podcasts

 Posted on February 15, 2024  |  1 minutes  |  201 words  |  Larita Shotwell

NEW YORK (CelebrityAccess) — iHeartMedia on Wednesday announced they are partnering with Jake Brennan, host and producer of the rock and roll true crime podcast “Disgraceland” to bring two additional seasons of the popular podcast to life.

Season 3 will feature 10episodes and will debut everywhere March 14 with part one of a two-part episode on Kurt Cobain and Courtney Love, part two will follow on March 19.

Subsequent new episodes will be released bi-weekly on Tuesdays beginning March 26 with an episode on Snoop Dogg.

In addition, “Disgraceland” will debut the Season 3 premiere via iHeartMedia’s network of broadcast radio stations across on March 13 before it’s available digitally on March 14.

The partnership will also include a new slate of iHeartRadio Original podcasts led by Brennan set to be released later this year. Two of the originals include “Rocka Rolla,” a serialized version of Disgraceland with a 10-episode arc on the madness and genius of Phil Spector, and “The 27 Club,” a new anthology series that will explore the lives of rock stars who died mysteriously at the age of 27.

The new shows will be hosted by Brennan and co-produced by iHeartRadio in the style of “Disgraceland.”
