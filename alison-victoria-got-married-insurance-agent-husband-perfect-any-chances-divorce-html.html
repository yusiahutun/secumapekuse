Alison Victoria, famously renowned for being the first female host of the DIY Network series Crashers, is also a top-notch interior designer and belongs to the category of few lucky people to have experienced a flawless wedding with her husband.

Let's figure out what made her wedding so perfect? And get some insight into her marital life.

Who is Alison Victoria's Husband?

A guy proposing to his girl, under the moonlight at an ideal destination, with a bottle of champagne for celebration sounds like something romantically fictional. But, things like that happen in reality, for fairy tales do come to life at times.

Romantically Married:- Matthew Rhode Age 36 Married, GOT Themed Romantic Wedding Ceremony

For all the guys, who are charmed by Alison's amazing personality, sorry she is no more single! Alison has already experienced an extreme amount of love from her husband, Luke Harding. 

Luke is an owner of the insurance agency, as he planned a grand proposal for her back in time. Alison, who had first met her husband through a dating website, was for the surprise of her life when Luke proposed to her in the most romantic way.

She described how it all went down and how she was left in awe the whole time, stating,

“We were in Paris, and we were having drinks at our hotel after dinner, At midnight, the concierge came out to us and said, ‘Mr. Harding, we have your car ready.’ He drove us to the Eiffel Tower with a blanket, Champagne and the biggest bag of chocolate truffles.

Luke proposed during our midnight picnic and said he wanted to wait for this night because it was his parents’ 34th wedding anniversary, and their marriage was something he wanted with me.”

Alison Victoria with Her Husband Luke Harding, at Their Engagement (source: chicagosplash.com)

She admitted it was the most romantic spot on the planet where Luke proposed to her.

Despite being an interior designer who makes sure about the things being perfect, she wasn't the girl who dreamt of her wedding for years and planned everything to perfection.

It was rather planned during a period when she was simultaneously hosting one of the most popular home-design shows on TV.

The couple together planned their wedding for 120-person, at The Drake Hotel in the year 2013. Describing her big day, Alison stated,

 “I wanted to keep it very classic, Our colors were white and pale blush with gold accents. But don’t let the demure colors fool you—this wedding was full of surprises. I know that weddings are really not about the bride and groom, It’s about everyone else, so we wanted to make sure it was a night to remember—and we definitely did that with the Sexy Sax Man.

For those that knew who he was, it was the greatest surprise ever—and for those that had never heard of him, like my 82-year-old father, it was the most terrifying and somewhat offensive moment of their lives!”

She wanted everyone to have an amazing night on their wedding, and as she desired, everything lived up to her expectation.

Soon after her wedding, Alison and her husband Luke became parents to a beautiful daughter, whose name is yet to be revealed.

More on Alison

Born to her parents on 31 October 1981, Victoria belongs to American nationality as she originally is a native of Chicago, Illinois.

Victoria did her graduation from the University of Nevada, Las Vegas. Her talent and immensely creative attribute contributed to the fame she possesses, which also rewarded her with the opportunity of hosting and designing the HGTV series Kitchen Crasher.

At the age of 40, she already is an owner and consultant of her firm, Alison Victoria Interiors. Further, in 2019, she also starred in Windy City Rehab's reality show, which features Alison as the lead cast.

The HGTV series had Alison visit around her hometown, Chicago, renovating old houses but keeping the originality of the homes alive. Further, she has also made cameo performances in shows such as Ultimate Crash: House, Bath, Kitchen and Yard, and Hotel Impossible.
