The 10 plagues of Turtle Creek Lane! Mormon influencer's $12M Dallas mansion has to be ripped apart

 Posted on April 29, 2024  |  8 minutes  |  1560 words  |  Larita Shotwell

A Mormon influencer who loves showing off her dazzling Dallas mansion has been plagued with an infestation of bees - after thousands were found living in the walls of the $12million super-home.

Jennifer Houghton, 55, took to social media to share the shocking discovery Wednesday - two years after she saw the manse on scenic Turtle Creek Lane flooded by a burst pipe during 2021's Arctic blast. The house also suffered floods earlier this year.

Houghton, whose husband Steve made his fortune in storage, shared grim clips of the bees being extracted from her daughter Tiffany's room, complete with custom harlequin-patterned walls.

And on Thursday, Houghton revealed an even bigger swarm had been found embedded in the walls of Steve's trophy hunting room, complete with stuffed zebra and animal heads on its walls.

On Wednesday, the blonde, big-haired designer who shuns sugar and wears a glucose monitor posted several stories to her more than 1million followers distinctly different from her usual home décor - showing how a hive had been secretly lining one of her home's rooms for what's likely been years.

A removal operation carried out by several licensed beekeepers, a vacuum, and an assortment of sting-proof suits ensued - with the self-made designer recording the entire ordeal for all to see.
