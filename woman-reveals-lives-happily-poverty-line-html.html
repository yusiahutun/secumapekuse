Woman reveals how she 'lives happily below the poverty line'

 Posted on August 19, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  1058 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

A supersaver has revealed how she has been living happily on $1,000 her entire adult life by employing a number of savvy tricks - including using a bidet over toilet paper and forgoing trips to the movies. 

Amber Storck, 42, from California, has more than 300,000 subscribers on YouTube where she shares details about her cost-cutting measures, using the handle @PrepperPrincess.

In one video, she shows how she can live on less than $30 worth of food a week by being smart about the ingredients she purchases.

Taking viewers on a shopping trip to Walmart, she stocks up on a ready-to-eat whole chicken, a pot of classic roast ground coffee, Raisin Bran, whole milk, 12 large eggs, milk chocolate brownie mix, carrots, celery, green beans, three naval oranges and a banana, with the total coming to $23.43.

Amber Storck's money saving measures

  • Buying groceries in bulk and making the same meals for a week
  • Using a bidet instead of toilet roll
  • Washing her hair once a week and only using a dime-sized amount of product 
  • Watering down whole milk to make it go further
  • Using free streaming services instead of paying for cable TV 
  • Doing home repairs herself
  • Wearing clothes until they are threadbare
  • Sourcing furniture for free from family or community groups 
  • Signing up to the local library for book and movie rentals 
  • Using a credit card for all transactions to easily track payments

When she gets home, she then shows how she can make these ingredients stretch a week.

Firstly, to make the milk last longer, she decants 25 per cent of it into another container and adds water to the original carton so that it's a slightly lighter consistency. 

Next she goes about taking all of the meat off the chicken and putting the carcass into a crock pot with carrot shavings to make a broth. 

For breakfast, Amber shows how she has a bowl of Raisin Bran with milk along with a cup of coffee. 

Meanwhile for lunch she makes deviled eggs with carrot and celery sticks, and half a piece of fruit. 

For dinner, there's shredded chicken, rice and green beans on the menu, with a chocolate brownie to finish. 

Many viewers applauded her cost-cutting grocery measures.

One fan commented: 'After watching this video, it's almost disgusting to me how much food I waste. My next shopping trip, I will definitely keep this in mind.' 

Another viewer put forward the suggestion of oatmeal or grits instead of cold cereal as they said it is 'even cheaper per serving and... much healthier than cold cereal.' 

To make her meals seem bigger, the penny pincher told The U.S. Sun that she uses a nine-inch plate instead of a 12-inch plate, 'which is what they did all the way until the 1970s.'

Any leftovers or scraps Amber has from cooking go to her two dogs.  

In her other videos, she reveals some of her other money-saving measures.

When it comes to her bathroom antics, the Californian says she only washes her hair once every five to seven days and when she does, she uses a 'dime-sized' amount of product.

This means a bottle of shampoo or conditioner can last her for 'probably two to three years.'

In a bid to save further, Amber doesn't use toilet paper at home and she has installed a bidet which she uses instead.

She previously revealed that she does have toilet paper out though in case guests visit and they are not accustomed to using a bidet. 

When it comes to her wardrobe, Amber says she wears her clothes until they are 'threadbare' and then when she does buy clothes, she will look for bargains at Walmart or Target.

While she used to shop for clothing at thrift stores, Amber says she has realized that the worn clothes won't last as long as new items and it's sometimes worth spending a few dollars more on fresh items. 

On the entertainment side of things, Amber avoids eating out and going to the movies, and she uses free streaming services instead of paying for cable TV.

She also recommends joining a local library as this is a cost effective way of accessing book and movie rentals. 

In terms of her housing costs, Amber lives mortgage free and she has a property in Arizona that she rents out for $1,500 a month.

The property she lives in has solar panels, which means she does not pay for any electricity.

She says all of the furniture in her houses was either donated by family or she picked it up for free by joining Facebook and local community groups. 

And when it comes to home repairs, Amber does everything herself instead of hiring a contractor.  

So she can keep track of her spending, Amber puts all of her transactions on a credit card. 

She explains: 'It gives me a really great visual presentation of my spending analysis, what exactly I'm spending my money on. 

'I pay it off every month so there is never any interest charges accumulating... and I get to earn rewards points.'

In one clip, Amber shows how her monthly spend comes in at $577.50 a month or $6,930 a year. 

Some of her biggest monthly outgoings include $30 for water, $26 for garbage removal, $70 on internet and $83 in property tax charges.

For more insights into Amber's frugal lifestyle her book, Living On Almost Nothing, is available via Amazon from $7.
