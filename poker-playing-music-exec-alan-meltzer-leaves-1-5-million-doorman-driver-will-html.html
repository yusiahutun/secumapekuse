A childless multimillionaire music executive reportedly left $1.5 million to his faithful doorman and driver when he passed away.

Alan Meltzer, also a prolific poker player, left $1 million to his chauffeur and $500,000 to his doorman after his sudden death last October.

The 67-year-old founder of Wind-Up Records had divorced his wife a year before his death and they had no children, the New York Post reported.

Generous: Alan Meltzer, founder of the world's largest independently-owned record label, left $1.5 million to his driver and doorman after his death in October

His beneficiaries were stunned by Meltzer's generosity, the New York Post reported.

Chauffeur Jean Laborde told the paper: 'I don’t know what to do exactly with the money, but one thing I know for sure - every year, I’m going to bring the guy some flowers at his grave.'

The 54-year-old father of five, from Irvington, New Jersey, added: 'He was such a nice guy. He left me money, but it’s not a good deal for me because it means he’s no longer here.'

His doorman Chamil Demirag from Brooklyn, New York told the Post: 'He was a generous guy. He was a really good friend of mine, and I was a good friend of his. It’s a surprise. Peace and rest to him. That’s all I can say.'

Poker pals: Meltzer, pictured seated far right, was also a well-known face on the poker circuit. He appeared on cable TV show High Stakes Poker in 2009

Ex-wife: Meltzer had divorced his wife Diana, pictured right with musicians Shaun Morgan and Amy Lee, a year before his death. The couple had no children

Meltzer founded New-York based Wind-Up Records with his wife, Diana in 1997.


'If he wants to give it to the bums, he can give it to the bums. He can give his money to whoever he wants. I could care less'

Diana Meltzer, ex-wife

Wind-up, the largest independently-owned record label in the world, has bands including Evanescence and Creed on its books.

Meltzer and his wife, a former model, divorced in 2010 after 13 years together.

Diana told the Post she 'could care less' that she received nothing of her former husband's $10 million fortune.

'He can leave it to whoever he wants to. I’m doing fine. I could care less,' she said.

Extravagant: Meltzer owned numerous lavish New York City properties in his lifetime, including this West End Ave address and a $27 million penthouse on the Upper East Side

'If he wants to give it to the bums, he can give it to the bums. He can give his money to whoever he wants. We’re divorced. The man is dead.'

The cause of Meltzer's sudden death is still unknown.

Before forming Wind-Up, Meltzer was a pretzel vendor on the streets of Brooklyn, New York.

He went on to form CD One Stop, a chain of record stores in New York and Connecticut.

The entrepreneur was also a well-known face on the poker circuit and appeared on cable television show High Stakes Poker in 2009.

He had owned a series of extravagant properties in his lifetime, including a $27 million penthouse in the celebrity-filled One Beacon Court on New York's Upper East Side.
