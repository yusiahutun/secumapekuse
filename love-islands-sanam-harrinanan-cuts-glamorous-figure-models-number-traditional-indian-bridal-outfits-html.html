Sanam Harrinanan looked nothing short of sensational while modelling a number of glamorous bridal outfits on Tuesday.

The Love Island winner, 25, commanded attention as she slipped into a stunning golden embroidered bralette with a coordinated full matching maxi skirt. 

She looked incredible in the eye catching number from Nikaza Asian Couture which she teamed with a chunky choker necklace and drop earrings.

The reality star styled her long dark tresses in a dramatic long braid as she held a coordinated scarf over her head. 

Swapping into a dazzling silver ensemble, Sanam seemed in high spirits as she twirled in the elaborate gown.

She put on a leggy display through a daring thigh-high split and added inches to her frame while wearing a pair of towering white pointed toe heels. 

While posing up a storm, she donned a number of traditional jewellery from Red Dot Jewels to complete the looks. 

Sanam - who has a mix of Indian and Trinidadian heritage, revealed that she had never really looked into her heritage before Love Island and had 'a bit of an identity crisis' because she didn't fit in.

She told OK! Magazine: 'I only really started to look into my heritage when I was on Love Island when people started to question if I was Indian or Caribbean - people didn't understand what Indo/Caribbean was. But I was starting to see so many positive comments from girls who finally felt represented!

'I have always felt like I've had a bit of an identity crisis because I haven't felt like I fit in with the Indian culture, but I also don't feel like I fit in with the Caribbean side either. So this was a wonderful way to find out more about myself and my Indian side. My mum was so excited for me when I told her I'd be dressing in modern embellished Lehengas and jewelled tops.'

The stunner went on to discuss that she is excited to take boyfriend Kai Fagan, who she won the winter ITV2 series with, to learn about her heritage. 

She continued: 'I was born in Trinidad and Tobago and I'm looking forward to taking myself and Kai over there to visit my family and show him more of my heritage and culture in the new year.'

Taking to Instagram following the photoshoot, Sanam shared a number of behind-the-scenes snaps and revealed she had to take several breaks due to being 'really hot and tired' from wearing the 'heavy' outfits. 

She penned: 'This was the behind-the-scenes of the shoot, the clothing was so heavy that I got really hot and tired and had to take drink breaks'.

The TV personality continued: 'One of the most exciting shoots I've done. So grateful to have been a part of this and honoured to have worn such beautiful traditional bridal wear. Thank you to everyone who made this shoot possible!' 

It comes after Sanam accused the show's producers of dumbing down the contestants by cutting their 'deeper' exchanges.

The reality star, who won the winter series in March alongside boyfriend Kai, told MailOnline she believes bosses need to air more of the meaningful conversations that take place as the Islanders date because they do happen but aren't broadcast.

Stars of the show are instead seen regurgitating phrases from past series including 'my type on paper' and 'can I pull you for a chat?' while Sanam says couples are connecting on a deeper level and she would talk to Kai about their families, future together and core values.

Sanam, who's a social worker from Bedford, fell in love with schoolteacher Kai, 25, after meeting on the ITV2 show and insists the couple are in it for the 'long haul' and she's been on 'cloud nine' since winning the competition.

She said: 'It shocked me because going on the show I didn't think that I would connect with someone on such a personal level. I didn't expect to go on Love Island and actually find love.

'When you watch Love Island you don't often see those conversations, so you don't think it's happening. But with me and Kai we would speak about our families, our values, future, we connected on a deeper level that I didn't think would happen in the villa.

'They need to show that more actually. We are a really good match because we connect on so many different levels.'

Sanam and Kai became the first non-white couple to win the show in Love Island's ten-year run after they connected during the Casa Amor loyalty test.

They also made history by Sanam being the only Casa Amor bombshell to go all the way in the competition after viewers fell in love with the pair's story, seeing that their romance was genuine.

And their relationship has thrived since leaving the villa, with Sanam saying the couple aren't in any rush to move in together but it already feels like they do due to the amount of time they spend in each other's company.
