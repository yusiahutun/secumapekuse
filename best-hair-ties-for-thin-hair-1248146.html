    How to Choose the Best Hair Tie for Thin Hair?

    1. Check the Width

    To choose the best hair tie for thin hair, ensure you’re getting a hair tie in the range of 2mm-5mm. Before tying your hair for a long time, ensure it’s neither tugging at your hair nor letting your hair become loose and sloppy. 

    2. Assess Stretchability

    Another important factor to consider before buying the best hair tie for thin hair is stretchability. Ensure the hair tie stretches properly to avoid tugging at your hair follicles, which can cause hair loss and headaches.

    3. Check the Design

    Design is also an important factor to consider before buying the best tie for thin hair. You may use spiral design hair ties for thin hair as they reduce friction to avoid the tangling of knots. Scrunchies, cotton, and woven ponytail holders may also make the thin hair more manageable.

    Our contributor and a hair expert, Eliza Pineda, says, “People with thin hair have more fragile strands and can be prone to breakage, tangling, frizz, and matting. It’s important to choose a hair tie that will be gentle on the strands. Stay away from thin elastics that can cause major breakage and tangling. Opt for softer materials such as satin or silk and go for a scrunchie design to avoid tangles and damage. Scrunchies, cotton, and woven ponytail holders may also make the thin hair more manageable.”

    How to Use Hair Ties for Thin Hair?

    Step 1: Brush your hair gently to detangle your knots.

    Step 2: Collect your hair in an upward direction for a high ponytail. You can choose accordingly for different hairstyles.

    Step 3: Use wide and soft hair ties for tying your hair.

    Step 4: Don’t tie your hair too tightly, as that may pull your strands and weaken them.

    Step 5: Wash your hair ties to protect your hair from infections.

    FAQ's

    Which hairstyle is suitable for thin hair?

    Bob cuts, layers, bangs, etc. are suitable for thin hair.

    Can hair ties prevent hair fall?

    Yes, hair ties can prevent hair fall by reducing stress on locks.

    Can hair ties damage your hair?

    Hair ties can damage your hair if they are made from rough material and lack proper stretchability.

    Is it better to sleep with your hair tied or open?

    Tying your hair gently while sleeping is better as it can protect your tresses from frizziness and hair loss.

    Are spiral hair ties better than scrunchies?

    Both spiral hair ties and scrunchies are good options for thin hair. However, scrunchies with smooth texture can make your hair feel at ease.

    Does tying hair in a ponytail help it grow?

    No, there is no evidence to prove that ponytails can enhance hair growth.

    Is a low or high ponytail better?

    It depends on your facial shape and what suits you better. However, you should switch between low and high ponytails as tying your hair in the same position for a long time can cause a receding hairline.

    Can tying your hair in a ponytail everyday cause damage?

    Tying your hair in a ponytail every day can make your tresses stressed.
