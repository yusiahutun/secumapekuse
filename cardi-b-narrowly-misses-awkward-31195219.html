American rapper Cardi B recently avoided an awkward nip slip by the narrowest of margins, as she shared a sultry selfie under her duvet.

The 31-year-old headed over to X, formerly known as Twitter, to post a breath-taking snap of herself posing up a storm while in bed. Wearing a glamourous diamonte necklace and a cap, Cardi smiled gleefully down the camera lens.

But as she held the sheets closely to her chest, the areola was exposed slightly on one of her boobs. Captioning the post, Cardi simply wrote, "Fine as can be" and added a flame emoji.

Another penned: "Pancake almost came out!" Meanwhile a third added: "Honestly, FINER THAN HUMANLY POSSIBLE."

Earlier this week Cardi, whose real name is Belcalis Marlenis Almánzar celebrated her 31st birthday. Turning heads along the way, the star headed out to dinner in a plunging red dress accompanied by her husband, rapper Offset.

The award-winning musician showed off her gorgeous jaw-dropping physique during the occasion in a long silky red gown which included floral lace detailing. Cardi paired her gorgeous look with red high heels, a matching red clutch bag and a diamond necklace.

Cardi wore her brunette hair in curls in a classy up-do look and allowed some of the spirals to sweep over her face. During their intimate dinner, Cardi opened her gifts from her husband which happened to be a number of luxury Hermes designer bags.

However, according to recent reports, Offset's three Hermes bags worth almost $500,000 caused uproar amongst animal rights organization PETA. This was partly due to the fact that the bags are known to include crocodile skins.

Celebrity Relations Assistant Manager Nicole Cummins wrote a strongly worded letter to Offset which was released by the Daily Mail. Some of the content from the letter read: "We saw that you gifted Cardi B three Hermès Birkin bags for her birthday. Everyone deserves a little luxury, but in this case, it came at a high price for animals.

"Crocodiles killed for the bags you bought are packed into severely crowded, barren concrete pits for months or even years before they’re finally slaughtered for their skin." The letter also stated: "The bags you purchased were once living, feeling individuals who endured unbearable pain, moment by moment, while they were being killed."
