Canadian actor, screenwriter, and producer Jared Keeso is best known for his role as the creator and star of the widely acclaimed comedy series Letterkenny, a show that earned him a Canadian Screen Award for Best Comedy Series in 2017. 

Similarly, Keeso has left a memorable mark on the Canadian television landscape with his award-winning performances, including his portrayal of Ben Chartier in the series 19-2. 

Noteworthy achievements in his career include winning a Canadian Screen Award for his role in 19-2, as well as receiving accolades for his depiction of hockey legend Don Cherry in the television films Keep Your Head Up Kid: The Don Cherry Story and The Wrath of Grapes: The Don Cherry Story II. 

How tall is Jared Keeso? Details on his height and weight

While Keeso’s exact height remains unconfirmed in the public domain, various reports suggest that the Canadian actor stands at around 5 feet 9 inches. 

Additionally, reports indicate that he maintains a weight of approximately 73 kilograms. Regardless of the specifics, his talents and on-screen charisma have made him a prominent figure in the entertainment industry.

Revealing Jared Keeso’s net worth and career 

Although the exact details of Keeso’s net worth remain undisclosed, Celebrity Net Worth estimates that the accomplished actor has amassed a substantial fortune, placing his net worth at an impressive $4 million.

Keeso portrayed the iconic hockey figure Don Cherry in two CBC television movies: Keep Your Head Up Kid: The Don Cherry Story in 2010 and its sequel, The Wrath of Grapes: The Don Cherry Story II, in 2012. 

His stellar performances in this role earned him accolades, including a Leo Award for Best Lead Performance by a Male in a Feature Length Drama and a Gemini Award for Best Performance by an Actor in a Leading Role in a Dramatic Program or Mini-Series.

In 2013, Keeso, along with Mike Borden, ventured into the digital realm by creating Play Fun Games Pictures on YouTube. The channel gained rapid success with its series of short sketches depicting the humorous aspects of the quintessential Canadian farmer and associated challenges, collectively known as Letterkenny Problems.

This online success served as the foundation for the 2016 sitcom Letterkenny, set and shot in Sudbury, Ontario, accumulating over 100 million views as of January 2022. 

Similarly, in 2014, he starred in Heritage Minutes, focusing on the Winnipeg Falcons, a team of Icelandic-Canadian hockey players who not only served in the First World War but also secured Canada’s first Olympic gold medal in hockey. 

Building on the cult success of Letterkenny, Keeso ventured into creating a spin-off series, Shoresy, in 2022, centering around the character Shoresy.

Notably, Keeso portrayed Ben Chartier in the 2014 English-language adaptation of 19-2, earning him a Canadian Screen Award for Best Performance by an Actor in a Continuing Leading Dramatic Role. 

Additionally, he took on diverse roles in films, such as Nicholas in Smokin’ Aces 2: Assassins’ Ball, Adam in the high-school-themed Lifetime original movie Seven Deadly Sins, and Simon in the drug-themed Charlie.

Is Jared Keeso missing a tooth?

Since its premiere in 2016, the Canadian hit series Letterkenny has garnered immense popularity for its humorous portrayal of rural life, filled with puns, wordplay, and hilarious vignettes. 

In 2022, Keeso, the creator and star of Letterkenny, introduced the spinoff Shoresy, centered around his beloved yet only partially-seen hockey player from the original series. 

Shoresy, known for his lightning-fast retorts delivered in falsetto with his face obscured, takes center stage in his own series, showcasing a devilish smirk with a missing tooth. 

The missing tooth became a subject of debate among fans on Reddit, where discussions revolved around whether Keeso wore makeup or was fully committed to the portrayal. 

However, as per The Canada Media Fund, Keeso had the tooth surgically removed to ensure authenticity. 

Exploring Jared Keeso’s early life

Keeso was born on July 1, 1984, in Listowel, Ontario, to his parents, Anne and Richard Keeso. His family owned and operated The Keeso Sawmill Company. 

In his youth, Keeso actively engaged in the family business, spending several years working at the sawmill. However, a significant turning point occurred on September 9, 2018, when a devastating fire destroyed the sawmill.

Despite this setback, Keeso, like many boys in the closely-knit community, discovered his passion on the hockey rink. 

The actor pursued a career in junior hockey, playing for the Strathroy Rockets in the WOHL, where he shared the ice with future two-time Stanley Cup champion Jeff Carter. Additionally, his hockey journey led him to the Listowel Cyclones of the GOJHL. 

Does Jared Keeso have any tattoos?

On his right bicep, Keeso proudly displays a ‘J.H. KEESO’ tattoo, an homage to the Sawmill company owned and operated by his father.

The J.H. KEESO business has a rich history, dating back to its founding in 1872 by Keeso’s ancestors, making the tattoo a symbol of familial legacy. Before embarking on his successful acting career, the actor spent a considerable portion of his life working in the mill. 

On his left bicep, Keeso sports a tattoo of his dog. The Letterkenny actor’s love for his friend is evident as fans have witnessed them on walks and in markets. To immortalize this companionship, Keeso chose to ink his dog’s portrait on his skin. 

Interestingly, amidst his collection of meaningful tattoos, Keeso also sports several random lines and word tattoos that may seem enigmatic to outsiders. While these may not have an obvious meaning for onlookers, they likely hold personal significance.
