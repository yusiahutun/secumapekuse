Kate Moss on Vivienne Westwood in new documentary

 Posted on July 7, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  233 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Kate Moss says that Vivienne Westwood once told her that the supermodel could have been her first and only lesbian lover.

Talking in a new documentary about the legendary designer entitled Westwood: Punk, Icon, Activist, out in cinemas today, Westwood told Moss backstage at a show that if she were gay, she'd be the woman she'd fall for.

"We were backstage and she went 'Kate, you know I've never been into girls, but I could have got into you,'" says Moss doing an unusual impersonation of Westwood's Yorkshire accent, which you can see for yourself with our exclusive clip above.

Getty Images

The documentary takes us inside Westwood's home, workshops and mind. We find out about her childhood, the mocking laughter she faced when she first debuted her boundary-smashing collections on TV and her pre-show tantrums.

You won't find old stories about punk and the Sex Pistols here (she finds all that terribly dull); Westwood is far more interested in the future and how to make the world better. In fact, the designer herself publicly denounced the film earlier this year because there isn't enough activism featured.

Don't let that put you off - even if the documentary falls short on giving Westwood's political campaigning that precedence it deserves, we still see the designer for who she is - a unique, passionate and game-changing fashion punk.
