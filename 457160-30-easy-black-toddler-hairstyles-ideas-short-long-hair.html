30 easy black toddler hairstyles ideas for short and long hair

 Posted on October 9, 2024  |  11 minutes  |  2155 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Styling a black toddler's hair is considered a hassle because of the unique nature of their hair. The first idea that could cross your mind is to have the hair cut short so that you can save yourself from the trouble of styling it. However, this should be your last resort because there are several easy black toddler hairstyles for short and long hair that are effortless to achieve.

When it comes to toddler hair styling, you need to know that they will not sit long enough to get a complicated hairstyle. Therefore, before you settle on a specific hairstyle, you should consider several aspects, such as the level of temperament, hair length, hair texture, and the amount of time you have on your hands.

From cornrows, buns, puffs, and sleek ponytails, there are several easy natural hairstyles that look amazing and cute on your little one. According to Hairstyle Camp, these are some of the easiest hairstyles for short and long hair.

1. Triple Mohawk

Mohawk is top on the list of simple hairstyles for a black toddler. The Mohawk isn't only a time-saving idea but also a trendy one. This style sets your little one apart from the crowd easily. To achieve this style, you only need to tie up your toddler's hair into three ponytails. Then, you can accessorize the ponytails using colourful bands or clips.

2. Cornrows

If you are looking for a 3-year-old hairstyle girl black, this is the perfect choice for you. This is because a three-year-old can sit through the cornrows. There are different cornrow hairstyles to choose from. You can accessorize the ends of the cornrows using beads or headbands.

3. Cute double buns with bubble bands

The double buns hairstyle never goes out of fashion. To achieve this style, you need to comb your toddler's hair thoroughly before pulling it up into cute double buns. Secure the buns with colourful accessories such as bands.

4. Braided ponytails

This is one of the easiest hairstyle ideas for black toddlers. You need to tie the hair into small ponytails . If you want the hairstyle to last longer, you should add a small amount of Kanekalon hair to each braided ponytail. You can accessorize the braided knot using coloured bands or beads.

5. Knotted turban

If you need to head out with your little one and you don't have the time to style her hair, the knotted turban is the perfect choice for you. You only need to throw the turban to cover the hair of your little one, and you are good to go.

6. Bantu knots

Bantu knots are the most common easy black little girl hairstyles. The origin of the Bantu knots can be traced back to the Zulu tribe of South Africa. This easy protective style can last for one week. To achieve this style, you need to section off the hair and wrap it into tiny buns. You can make a few or many knots depending on the hair length and the toddler's temperament.

7. Big afro

This is one of the cutest looks on black toddler girls. If your toddler has healthy, thick hair, you can let her flaunt an afro. You need to apply plenty of conditioner to prevent the hair from frizzing out.

8. Side part with bow

If you are heading out with your toddler and do not have enough time to style her hair, this is the perfect option. You only need to part off her hair on the side and attach a big bow for cuteness. The style looks perfect on toddlers with naturally curly hair.

9. Twist ponytails

If your toddler has thick long hair, you can style it into three ponytails, two at the front and one at the back. Then, make several twists out of the hanging hair. You can accessorize the ends of the twists using colourful beads.

10. High ballerina bun

The ballerina bun hairstyles are usually short and rounded. However, the high ballerina bun is longer. To achieve this look, you should comb the hair of your little one into a ponytail and wind it around the base. You can accessorize the bun using colourful hairbands.

11. Mini puffs

This hairstyle is perfect for toddlers who have short hair. The style allows you to flaunt your own sense of creativity. All you need to do is pull up your little girl's hair into tiny little puffs and secure them with colourful hair accessories such as coloured beads or bands.

12. Natural hair with a headband

One colourful headband can turn your toddler's natural hair into something neat and trendy. The headband will hold her natural hair into place, and it will not allow it to hang loosely on her forehead.

13. Cornrows with bun

This easy black toddler hairstyle looks adorable, and it never gets out of style. All you need to do is plait simple cornrows and join them together at the crown to form a beautiful hair bun on top. Once you are done, you can accessorize it with a colourful band to secure the bun.

14. Messy curly hair

If you are really struggling to work on your toddler's hair, you should let her flaunt in messy curly hair. This style gives volume to your toddler's hair. The curls look super cute on black toddlers who have voluminous hair. However, you can give the hair extra volume and bounce using a colourful well-placed headband.

15. Twisted black hair

Twists are an exceptional look and an ideal black toddler hairstyle. To achieve this style, you need to part her hair into sections and braid it into twists. You can secure the braids with beads to finish the hairstyle.

16. Braided ropes with cherries and floral accessories

The braided ropes are the easiest styles to achieve for black toddlers. For this style, you should part the hair and create beautiful sections that serve as the origin of the cool braided ropes. Then, accessorize each section with cherries or other floral accessories. Your little one will look gorgeous in this hairstyle.

17. Bubble bands

Bubble bands are the easiest toddler hairstyles. To achieve this style, you should begin by combing the hair of your little one all the way through before you make several ponytails all around her head. Secure each ponytail with colourful hair accessories and add a few beads to the ends.

18. Space buns with braids

Space buns are the favourite toddler hairstyles of all time. This hairstyle never goes out of fashion. To achieve it, you need to style your toddler's hair into two buns and leave some hair near the face for plaiting two thin braids. You can decorate the braids using accessories such as beads or hair cuffs.

19. Jumbo braids

This is one of the most famous black toddler girl hairstyles. For this look, you should separate the hair into several strands and plait a few jumbo braids. You can accessorize the braids using coloured accessories if you are attending an occasion. You can collect the braids on top into a bun or leave them hanging loose.

20. Short afro with a cute bow

This is one of the most common natural hairstyles for little black girls with medium or small hair. For this style, you will need to spritz the hair of your little girl with a leave-in conditioner. Use your fingers to fluff the hair and then accessorize it using a cute bow.

21. Double mega puffs

This is a cute hairstyle for a toddler who has voluminous hair. All you need to do is pull her hair into cute double puffs. You can accessorize the puffs with bows or a headband. The hairstyle does not last long, and you might be forced to restyle the hair every morning.

22. Double puffs with a love heart part

If you are looking for the simplest hairstyle to replicate, you should style your toddler's hair into double puffs and part some hair on the side to create the love heart as shown above. Before creating this style, you need to moisturize the hair with a leave-in conditioner. Accessorize the puffs with colourful bands or clips.

23. Cornrows with puff

If you have basic braiding skills, you should try this hairstyle on your little girl. All you need to do is braid the front part of her hair into simple braids. Pull the hair at the back into a puff. For short hair, you can add extensions to finish off the style.

24. Triple knots with Fulani braids

This is one of the easiest hairstyles for a 2-year-old black girl. To achieve this style, all you need to do is pull up your toddler's hair into three chunky knots. Add a few cornrows to complete the look. You can accessorize the Fulani braids with beads.

25. Sleek topknot

Tying your toddler's hair into a clean and tight topknot has the power to beat all the other black girl hairstyles. You only need to tie her hair into a tight topknot using a coloured band or any other accessory to achieve this look. You can use a hairspray to give the stray hair a neat look.

26. Side swept cornrows with braids

This hairstyle is perfect for your little princess as long as she can sit patiently and wait for the cornrows to be completed. Braid the side hair into cornrows and then plait the remaining hair into as many braids as possible. You can accessorize the braids using coloured beads or bands to give your toddler a chic look.

27. Double puffs with front braids

This is one of the easiest hairstyles you need to try on your little one this year. To achieve this look, you can plait two short braids in front, then gather the rest of her hair into two sleek pony puffs to let her natural hair poof out.

28. Neat pigtails

Pigtails are the easy go-to hairstyles for toddlers with long or medium hair length. The style does not require you to have any braiding skills. You only need to make perfectly straight lines as you part your toddler's hair. The pigtails can run in any direction. This style looks even cuter when accessorized using colourful bands or clips.

29. Half up, half down hairstyle

If your toddler's hair is super thick and curly, you can try this hairstyle on her. The half updo hairstyle features a low and high ponytail. You can make the top hair stand out by accessorizing it using coloured clips or bands.

30. Frohawks

If you thought that the frohawk style is for boys, you are wrong. Frohawks are a popular style among black toddlers because they are easy to create. The frohawk hairstyle features slicked down or braided sides and a curly strip of hair down the middle of the head. This style can suit any black toddler with medium to long hair.
