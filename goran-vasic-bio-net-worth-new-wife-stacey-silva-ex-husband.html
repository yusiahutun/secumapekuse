Meet Goran Vasic, Stacey Silva Ex Husband

Also, Stacey mentioned that Goran had a thick Florian accent, and didn’t speak English fluently back then.

So, dating for while the duo then went on to get married and have two kids named — Parker and Mateo Vasic.

Their son, Parker was born on September 9, 2004, and is a student-athlete. He’s been into sports for a long time and worked as a coach at Goran Vasic Soccer Academy.

As for their older kid, Mateo was born on August 7, 2021. Like his brother, Mateo is also into sports. But although he was on Xavier High School’s wrestling team, his true passion was writing.

The kids didn’t prefer to appear on the show. Rumors had that the two kids lived with their dad, although the parents shared the custody of their sons after the divorce.

Goran Vasic Job

Goran Vasic is a soccer coach who turned his successful soccer career into business after acquiring USSF “A” Coaching License in 2008.

However, it was April 2006 when Goran started Goran Vasic Soccer Academy. His academy now has been lighting up the ranks of professional soccer players with its training, clinics, and summer camp facilities.

Reportedly, a GVSA summer camp program per person costs around $135.

Besides GVSA, Goran was also working as the executive director of Sporting CT, and the director of travel soccer of Middletown Magic Soccer Club as of 2022.

Talking about his soccer career, Goran has over 20 years of experience as a professional player in Europe. This talented soccer player has been kicking soccer ever since he was 5. And by 8, he was already scouted by Red Star Belgrade, one of the largest clubs in Europe.

So, Goran remained in their youth system for the next 10 years until he was offered a deal to play for First Division and Second Division clubs in Serbia.

Thus, in May 1997, Goran move to the United States to play professionally for the Houston Hotshots of the Continental Indoor Soccer League. The next year, he then joined the Crunch/Force and spent another four years with the Philadelphia KiXX.

As for his indoor stats, Goran has a total of 675 points on 218 goals and 268 assists, with defensive prowess 183 blocks.

Back in 2016, he even to be the head coach for Next-Gen 2004 boys team at the Barcelona Spain MIC tournament.

This super coach even made it to the Connecticut Soccer Hall of Fame in 2020.

Trivia: Goran’s academy GVSA was a proud sponsor of Middletown Youth Soccer.
