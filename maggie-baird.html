Maggie Baird Biography

Maggie Baird is an American actress, voice artist, and screenwriter who was born in Fruita, Colorado as Maggie May Baird. She is best known as the mother of musicians Finneas and Billie Eilish.

Baird grew up in Colorado performing music, and studied theater as well as dance at the University of Utah, prior to moving to New York City where she performed on Broadway. She made her television debut in the year 1981 in the soap opera Another World and her film debut in An Innocent Man in 1989.

Baird continued to act in television and film after moving to Los Angeles in the year 1991 and became a member as well as a teacher at The Groundlings, a troupe and Improvisational theatre school. She acted in the year 2000s television series such as Bones, The X-Files as well as Six Feet Under.

In the year 2009, Baird released her debut studio album, We Sail. In the year 2013, Baird wrote as well as starred in her own film, Life Inside Out. In the year 2016, she edited the music video for “Six Feet Under” for her daughter, Billie Eilish.
