Ted Kravitz dodges kiss from Ferrari engineer after having cake whacked in his face

 Posted on May 30, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  466 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Sky Sports F1 star Ted Kravitz dodged a cheeky peck from a Ferrari engineer after getting a face full of birthday cake in the Australian Grand Prix paddock.

Kravitz, a mainstay of F1 coverage in the UK for over 20 years, was celebrating his 50th birthday Down Under during race weekend. A popular figure around the paddock, Kravitz was offered a cake by Ferrari engineer Diego Loverno, who would have been in a decent mood himself after watching Carlos Sainz romp to victory at Albert Park.

Kravitz was approached by Loverno, Ferrari's sporting director, who was holding a big chocolate cake. He insisted that someone should whack the cake into his face in line with tradition, sparking agreement from Loverno and Sky Sports F1 commentator David Croft.

"Normally people's heads end up in cakes," Kravitz claimed. "Am I wrong? In tradition, when it is somebody's birthday, do you not just want to put a little bit in my face?" Once the birthday cake had been smeared across his face, Kravitz turned to Loverno and went: "In the greatest honour of Formula One birthdays, you have to put a bit on my face", reports the Express.

However, something must have gotten lost in translation as Loverno said: "I may kiss you." Loverno leaned into Kravitz, who wasn't having any of it. "No, no, no! But congratulations [on the victory] and thank you for the cake," he replied.

It was also a terrible weekend for Mercedes, as neither of their drivers managed to finish even though they had a great opportunity to compete for the podium. Lewis Hamilton had to stop at the side of the track because of a serious power unit failure.

While George Russell was involved in a big crash on the final lap while he was trying to overtake Fernando Alonso for sixth place. He lost control of his back tyres just before turn six and crashed into the barriers, causing his car to flip onto its side. Also was demoted to eighth after the race.
