Every Lyric to Beyonc's Bodyguard, Explained

 Posted on February 24, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  687 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Beyoncé’s singing and songwriting capabilities are unmatched in their versatility, as she so clearly displays on her newly released eighth studio album, Cowboy Carter. But the international icon also never fails when it comes to delivering a classically great love song.

She brings us one with “Bodyguard,” a bouncy dance anthem that illustrates her desire to protect and care for her lover—so much so that she playfully offers to be their “bodyguard,” “lifeguard,” and “Kevlar” (the material used to make bulletproof vests).

The lyrics also detail her passion for and obsession with her partner, highlighting both the physical bond she feels and her feelings of jealousy. In the first pre-chorus, she sings, “Sometimes I hold you closer / Just to know you’re real / Wheels in the gravel / Davis in my bones / Sometimes I take the day off / Just to turn you on.” In the second verse, she adds, “I don’t like the way she’s lookin’ at you / Someone better hold me back, oh-oh / Chargin’ ten for a double and I’m talkin’ / I’m ’bout to lose it, turn around and John Wayne that ass.”

The song makes generous reference to riding shotgun in the car with her partner, a position that symbolically validates her feeling of primacy in her partner’s life. Riding shotgun is also mentioned in “II Most Wanted,” another Cowboy Carter love song, which features Miley Cyrus.

It’s likely that “Bodyguard” is an ode to Beyoncé’s husband, Jay-Z, but it also references western cinema. In a press release, the singer revealed that every song on the album reimagines a western, with movies like The Hateful Eight, Space Cowboys, and Killers of the Flower Moon inspiring her songwriting process.
