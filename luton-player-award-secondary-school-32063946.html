Fans love Luton's player of the month trophy that's 'like being back in secondary school'

 Posted on July 20, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  631 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Luton Town's player of the month trophy has been likened to a school trophy after Ross Barkley posed with the award.

The Premier League club awarded the honour to midfielder Ross Barkley for December with the former England midfielder having scooped up the prize in November as well thanks to his impressive performances for the relegation-threatened side.

The 30-year-old posed with the trophy in a photo that was posted to Luton's official X (formerly known as Twitter) last week with the caption: "Only one man was winning the Diamond Player of the Month award for December. Ain’t nobody..."

With the post having been viewed almost two million times, it didn't take long before fans commented on the classic-looking player of the month trophy that Barkley had won for two consecutive months.

A third echoed their sentiments by commenting: "Used to get one like that for the most house points at primary school". Barkley's experienced a career renaissance at Kenilworth Road since joining the club on a free transfer last summer and has been instrumental in their fight against relegation this season.

While the club had a difficult start to life in the Premier League with just two wins in their first 16 league games, manager Rob Edwards has turned fortunes around as his side have suffered just one defeat in their last six fixtures.
