25 best African paintings of all time

 Posted on September 23, 2024  |  8 minutes  |  1553 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Painters express many things through their paintings. From world famous painters like Leonardo da Vinci to Pablo Picasso, their paintings have many encoded details than what meets the eye. The same applies to African painters and their African paintings. Check out the following collection of paintings of all time from Africa.

From political and social-economic issues to race and architecture, there are dozens of arts that represent vintage and modern African paintings. Find out why great pieces of art by painters like Irma Stern, Jacob Hendrik Pierneef, and Julie Mehretu feature in this list.

List of the best African art paintings

Here is a list of African paintings that are popular for various reasons but mainly because of the genius and artistry behind them.

Les Chadoufs by Mahmoud Said

Les Chadoufs is by an Egyptian artist called Mahmoud Said. It is Said’s best painting based on its iconography, geometry, and price tag. Mahmoud did his best in this painting to literally paint the history of Egypt during the Egyptian Renaissance.

This masterpiece is one of the most expensive African paintings. It was sold for over $2 million.

Retopistics: A Renegade Excavation by Julie Mehretu

In the words of Julie Mehretu, this painting aims at depicting different narratives. From a distance, the Retopistics: A Renegade Excavation looks like a cosmology. However, at a closer look, lovers of paintings will appreciate the artistry and genius that went into making this painting to make it show numerous stories and events.

This masterpiece of densely layered abstract paintings by this Ethiopian-born was sold for $4.6 million at Christie’s New York in 2013.

Construction of the Suez Canal by Abdul Hadi El-Gazzar

Construction of the Suez Canal is a watercolor and gouache masterpiece by a renowned Egyptian painter.

Socio-political issues after the Revolution in 1952 and the Suez Canal Crisis were the main things that inspired El-Gazzar in 1965 to paint this piece.

The Teacher by Marlene Duma

Paintings can speak volumes. The Teacher by a South African South African artist and painter Marlene Duma is a classic example. Marlene got her inspiration from a class photo that was taken during the apartheid era.

This painting is provocative as it depicts the political status of South African during apartheid when there was widespread race discrimination.

Two Arabs by Irma Stern

Two Arabs is an oil-on-canvas painting by Irma. It is in the list of African expensive paintings. This piece of art earned Irma the top spot in 2011 as the most expensive painting ever sold in South Africa at $1.5 million.

Arab Priest by Irma Stern

Arab Priest is one of the most popular Irma Stern paintings. The most outstanding thing about this painting besides its artistic point of view is that it was donated to fund Nelson Mandela’s legal defense in the 1950s.

Just like other Irma paintings, Arab in Black was inspired by Irma’s visit to Zanzibar.

This painting was sold for $1.2 million.

Bahora Girl by Irma Stern

Irma Stern was a South African and well-known and major artist. Her pieces are dominating this list. Bahora Girl is yet another masterpiece by this artist. This painting was a depiction of the people she met while traveling through Zanzibar. Such depictions intrigued the Western world.

Bahora Girl was sold for R25.5m.

Self-Portrait of Suffering by El-Salahi

This El-Salahi painting can easily be listed in the category of beautiful African paintings because of its swirling and delicate lines that compose gaunt figures. This painting is a true depiction of self reflection and realizing what he had failed to see in his life.

Pouvoir et Religion (Power and Religion) by Abdoulaye Konaté

Power and Religion showcased at the Rivington Place arts space in Shoreditch. This textile art shows the deep symbolism and traditional craftsmanship of Konaté.

A quick interpretation of this art is the ambiguous positions that heads of governments have to take regarding religion.

Seated N*de with Oranges by Irma Stern

Given the influence of Irma Stern on African paintings, Seated N*de with Oranges is among famous African paintings.

This piece of art is the best example of how Stern was deep into the theme of fertility and regeneration. The painting also shows a compelling and distinctive use of color.

The painting was sold for $2.2 million.

Farm Jonkershoek with Twin Peaks Beyond, Stellenbosch by Jacob Hendrik Pierneef

Farm Jonkershoek with Twin Peaks Beyond, Stellenbosch is another piece of art that is in the list of expensive African paintings. It is also among Vintage African paintings because it was painted in 1928.

Chinese Girl by Vladimir Tretchikoff

The artistry behind this painting speaks for itself. Besides being among a collection of Vladimir Tretchikoff paintings that earned him a South African nationality, Chinese Girl features in the music video Dead Leaves, David Bowie’s The Stars Are Out Tonight music video‚ in a Don Paterson poem God’s Gift To Women‚ The Dirty Ground, and in the film Frenzy.

The original Chinese Girl painting fetched over R17 million.

The Pink Sari by Irma Stern

The Pink Sari is incredibly another painting by Irma that sold over R17 million under the hammer. This painting continues to show the inspiration that Stern was getting while in Zanzibar.

The pink sari is a stunning example of the beauty Stern encountered on the island. In the women of the Zanzibar in particular, she had found her greatest inspiration.

Young Arab by Irma Stern

Traveling is inspiring. This statement is true based on how the two trips to Zanzibar by Irma in 1939 and 1945 inspired the Young Arab painting.

Gladioli by Irma Stern

Gladioli should be among your favorite African art paintings if you love flower paintings.

You will appreciate the fondness for flowers by Irma. This canvas painting has appealing soft colors. At the same time, the color groupings create a nice combination that makes this painting exciting.

Extensive Landscape with Farmlands by Jacob Hendrik Pierneef

Jacob painted this piece after his visit to Europe. This painting shows what he had seen. The balance between light and pure colors of Impressionist and Post Impressionist, this painting reflects the joy, enthusiasm, and understanding he had had from the trip.

Jacob’s great passion is clear on this large canvas painting. You can also see how Jacob interpreted the landscape through with a structural bias.

Malay Girl by Irma Stern

Irma Stern painted Malay Girl in 1938. This painting contains all the elements that define Irma Stern as one of the best in Africa.

Malay Girl is one her most compelling portraits. It shows the face of a girl who on a closer look portrays a curious life of a child.

Extensive Landscape Northern Transvaal by Jacob Hendrik Pierneef

Extensive Landscape Northern Transvaal is another classic example that shows Jacob was intrigued by structural design.

White Lilies by Irma Stern

White Lilies was exhibited at Argus Gallery in 1941 hence does not fall in the category of the latest African paintings. During the exhibition, this painting was identified as among the best pieces.

Still Life of Delphiniums by Irma Stern

Still Life of Delphiniums is another South African painting that made another record. It broke the R200, 000-barrier for a painting from South Africa in the 20th century. The record only stood for five years.

Arab by Irma Stern

When checking for paintings from Africa, Arab by Irma Stern will feature in a list of the greatest African paintings. The fact that Arab was one of the most-sought after paintings since its creation in the 1930s is enough proof for the artistry behind it.

In this painting, it is clear that Stern focused on a single Arab. The face is contemplative, infused with kindness and bathed in glowing light.

Under the hammer, this painting fetched over R17 million.
