List of best TV brands in Kenya in 2024: the best TVs for your home

 Posted on May 20, 2024  |  7 minutes  |  1467 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Are you planning to upgrade your TV? Electronic shops around the country have many brands. Some models are expensive, while others look cheap. The market saturation may make it hard to select an ideal unit as an upgrade. Luckily, the best TV brands in Kenya are not necessarily expensive or from popular companies.

Unlike before, there are many TV companies that supply their products to the Kenyan market. Most of these companies offer great televisions that are reliable and display high-quality images. While it has its advantages, this large number of brands makes it harder for consumers to settle on an ideal choice. However, it can be easier for you if you know a few reliable brands.

The best TV brands in Kenya in 2024

So, which is the best TV brand in Kenya? It depends on what you want to do with your TV and the amount you want to spend. Your budget may also limit you to certain TV brands. Considering all these factors, the best nine brands that are available in the country are:

1. Samsung

The South Korean brand has been in the Kenyan market for decades selling different TVs for different customers. Like in the global market, Samsung is the best-selling TV brand in Kenya.

The main reason why Samsung is unmatched is its tradition of making quality TVs. While the brand’s high-end sets have not yet adapted OLED panels, the QLED displays have impressive brightness and high-contrast pictures.

Besides, Samsung has the best quality televisions on all price points. Whether you are buying a budget brand or the latest QLED set, you are assured of quality panels and unmatched built quality. Furthermore, all the new Samsung TVs come with Bixby.

2. LG

LG is popular for its midrange and flagship sets. Unlike most generic brands, LG makes some of the best panels. So, if you are looking for a set for your home or an additional monitor to your gaming set-up, it is worth the investment.

The sets are also extremely thin and light. While there are some slim TV options under Ksh 30,000, they cannot match the South Korean brand. Also, the brand has one of the smoothest OS in the smart TV market. The built design and OS make this brand ideal for many users.

Besides LG’s impressive specs across different price points, it is one of the best TV brands in terms of easiness to repair. Therefore, if your TV breaks down, many technicians in Kenya can repair the set professionally.

3. Sony

Sony is the most reliable TV brand, thanks to the company’s experience in making TVs. Even with the saturation of TV brands in the Kenyan market, Sony’s audio and picture combination are unrivalled. Sony is, therefore, a perfect choice for everyday usage and as gaming monitors.

While Sony’s midrange sets are not as good as most Chinese brands, they are durable. The choice of materials and designs are the key features that make Sony’s TVs worth buying. Besides, they have in-built Android decoders.

Unlike most brands that sell TVs through independent sellers, Sony has a footing in Kenya. The company’s presence in Kenya makes it easier to repair and replace faulty sets. Also, most professional technicians in Kenya are familiar with the brand and, therefore, repairing your set is easier.

4. TCL

TCL is a midrange TV make and one of the best TV brands in Kenya. It has impressive sets but with midrange price tags. The Chinese TV brand is a perfect option for users transitioning from standard TVs to the smart ecosystem but with a tight budget. The C-series is a perfect example of TCL’s brilliance in making great sets but at a midrange price.

Based on price and picture quality, TCL C75 is arguably the best smart TV in Kenya. Besides thin bezels and unmatched AI integration, the TV comes in different sizes. The colour accuracy also makes it perfect for all uses such as everyday TV use, gaming screen and streaming.

5. Hisense

Hisense is a Chinese electronic brand specializing in different electronics. The Qingdao-based company concentrates on making midrange and budget TVs.

Hisense TVs have decent black levels, sharp images and unmatched colour balance. Like most companies making TVs for the midrange market, the panels are decent and perfect for all types of uses. You can use a Hisense as the main home TV set or as a gaming monitor.

While some HDR Hisense may not match high-end brands in terms of brightness, they are reasonably priced. Over the years, the company has become synonymous with its aggressive pricing. You can buy a decent Hisense TV set in Kenya at a good price.

6. Haier

Are you looking for quality TVs under Ksh 25,000? If yes, Haier has some impressive options for you. The newer smart TVs, for example, give you a better experience, almost similar to high-end brands such as Samsung and LG.

Compared to other Chinese brands making cheaper TVs, Haier is the best. The panels have better colour accuracy. Also, the TVs are loud enough. Therefore, if you are on a tight budget, you can comfortably watch Haier without additional sound accessories.

Besides specs and a better OS, Haier has one of the best warranty offers. However, if the TV develops a problem outside the warranty period, many technicians in Kenya repair Haier items including the TVs.

7. Skyworth

Skyworth is a Chinese brand making different TVs for different consumers. It has cheaper, midrange and high-end TVs. Regardless of the TV’s size and specs; Skyworth has an aggressive approach to pricing. Also, if you want the cheapest TV in Kenya, buying any of the Skyworth TV models is the perfect decision.

Skyworth’s UHD TVs are incredible in two ways. The brand’s brightness and contrast ratio make the final picture great, especially when watching high-resolution movies. Second, the software is smooth, and you can use the set for more than one thing. While Skyworth’s sound may not match its impressive picture quality, you can use it with any speakers.

8. Bruhm

Bruhm is a Chinese TV brand designing and manufacturing affordable sets. Since 2015, the brand has become synonymous with affordable 24 and 32-inch TVs. While it also has high-end curved smart TVs, they are not as popular as other known brands.

Besides the brand popularity in Kenya, Bruhm’s budget TVs have unmatched features. For example, they have free inbuilt decoders. So, regardless of where you are in Kenya, you can watch hundreds of free-to-air channels. Also, you can connect the sets to any third-party decoders.

If you are looking for the best picture quality in a budget TV, Bruhm TVs are worth trying. The panels are decent, even though they may not match high-end brands’ colour accuracies. Also, the TVs have an elegant design, which gives them a premium look.

9. Vitron

Vitron is an affordable TV brand with decent specs for everyday use. Even though some TVs made by the Chinese company are unimpressive in terms of overall design and picture clarity, the sets are a perfect option for most Kenyans.

Though Vitron TV brand is one of the latest brands in the Kenya market, it has great sets for different users. The sound excellence and the quality of the panels make the TVs perfect for daily use and normal displays.

Vitron also has more connectivity ports. So, you can use the inbuilt or external decoder to watch TV. Unfortunately, the OS may be sluggish in some instances.
