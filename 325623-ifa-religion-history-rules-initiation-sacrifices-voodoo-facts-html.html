Ifa religion is one of the traditional practices among the Yoruba people of West Africa. The ancient religion has been in existence for thousands and thousands of years. What do you know about this religion?

Ifa religion is an indigenous and earth-centered African spiritual practice. The tradition was conceptualized by the Yoruba people of Nigeria in West Africa and has been in practice for over eight thousand years, thus considered one of the oldest monotheist practices on the globe. The foundation of the religion stands on three legs Orunmila, Orisa and the ancestors. Learn more about the spirituality in it and how to join Ifa religion.

Ifa religion history

The history and origins of Ifa religion is a very complex narrative woven with legends, myths, and folktales. It contains a rich philosophical and ethical epistemology rooted in Yorùbá cultural life and nature. There exist myths explaining the Yoruba creation story and origin of Ifa religion.

According to Yoruba mythology, Ifa was founded by Orunmila in the city of Ile-Ife (believed to be the origin of the Yoruba people). Another version has it that the religion was brought by Setiu (a Nupe man) to Ile-Ifa when he settled there. Another mythology suggests that it was introduced to Oyo by Arguba. She was the mother of Onibogi, the 8th Alaafin of Oyo. Arguba initiated Alado of Ato and gave him the rites to initiate others to him. He would then initiate priests in Oyo Empire.

What does the Yoruba religion believe?

In the Yoruba culture, the system is centered on Oludumare as their supreme being. He is without gender and is not an active participant in the affairs of human beings. He lives in Orun or heaven. The supreme being has provided to the universe all that humans need to live a happy and fulfilled life.

A sense of interdependence characterizes this religion. According to the religion, every life form and element of nature such as rocks, rivers, thunder, wind, name them have an inner soul force. These natural forces which form the universe are referred to as Orisa, and each one of them has a specific function. The Yoruba gods or Yoruba religion Orisha are as follows:

  • Orisa Yemonja or Olokun: The force of the sea or oceans (natural water bodies)
  • Orisa Esu: The energy of opportunity or chance
  • Orisa Oya: The force of the wind and sudden changes.
  • Orisa Ogun: The force of the forest. It is also the spirit of dynamic creativity. It is associated with blacksmiths
  • Orisa Osun: The energy of love and attraction of every good thing on all levels.
  • Orisa Sango: The force of thunder.

Ifa religion has a spiritual and natural world. The spiritual world is where Oludumare and Orisa live and the natural world is where human beings, plants, and animals live. It is through Ifa prayer, divination, spiritual procession and sacrifice that human beings can engage the spiritual world.

Two supernatural forces exist according to this religion, malevolent and benevolent forces. The benevolent are good supernatural powers that help human beings in their day to day activities on earth, and these are the gods "Orisa" and the ancestors. On the other hand, the evil forces are powers that work against the efforts of human beings. They constitute witches and the Ajogun (belligerent enemies of man).

Is Yoruba religion voodoo?

You may wonder is Ifa religion Voodoo? Well, Voodoo is a religious practice more common in Haiti and Haitians residing in other parts of the world such as America. Even though the two are practiced in different parts of the world, they have a common center of origin being Nigeria, Togo, and Benin. Cuban Santeria; Haitian Vodun (Voodoo); Brazilian Candomble; Trinidad and Tobago Shango are all Yoruba or Orisha based.

What is Odu Ifa?

Odu Ifa is patterns of binary codes (open and closed nodules) that represents all the energies contained in the Universe. They hold the keys to all blessings and misfortunes of life and death. Odu are oracles of the Yoruba divination systems of Obi, Ifa, and Merindinlogun. The mysteries and teachings of Ifa which are revealed in the divination are contained in Odu. It is through these energies that priests (babalawos) and priestesses (Iyanifasl) of Orisa can help people better themselves. Ifa religion sacrifice

Sacrifices are offerings made for a higher purpose. Just like many traditional religious practices, sacrifice forms an integral part of the tradition of Ifa. The Yoruba people believe that the solutions to their problems can only be meted out through the consultation with Ifa and that this is only possible through sacrifices. Sacrifices could be in the form of objects, food, or life and blood of animals.
