Prince Harry and Meghan Markle Attend Invictus Games 2017

 Posted on September 9, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  358 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Prince Harry and Meghan Markle have just reached a huge relationship milestone. Following recent speculation and a reported meeting with the Queen, the couple was spotted at the Invictus Games—however, they were not seated together.

Harry was seated beside First Lady Melania Trump, whom he met with earlier today, and by Prime Minister of Canada, Justin Trudeau.

Getty Images

Prince Harry (top row, second to the left) seated beside Melania Trump at the Opening Ceremony of the 2017 Invictus Games.

Markle was seated in a nearby section, according to multiple reports. She was seen sitting next to her friend, Markus Anderson, a fellow Canadian and consultant to Soho House Group, who is reportedly the one who introduced Markle to Prince Harry. The actress is wearing a purple leather jacket over a plum dress.

Getty Images

Getty Images

The 2017 games, which honor injured servicemen and women, are taking place in Toronto, where the actress has recently been busy filming the latest series of Suits. The public outing marks Markle's first step into the royal spotlight and it's likely to spark further rumors around an engagement announcement in the near future.

Although this moment is a first for the couple, Harry and Meghan haven't been shy about revealing their feelings for each other. Soon after news of their relationship broke, Harry released an official statement about the relationship through Kensington Palace in November 2016. More recently, Meghan opened up about dating the prince in her interview for the October issue of Vanity Fair.

She told the magazine: “We're a couple. We're in love. I'm sure there will be a time when we have to come forward and present ourselves and have stories to tell, but I hope what people will understand is that this is our time. This is for us. It's part of what makes it so special, that it's just ours. But we're happy. Personally, I love a great love story.”

During his time at the Games, Prince Harry will meet participants from all over the world, visit training sessions and give a speech at the Closing Ceremony on September 30.
