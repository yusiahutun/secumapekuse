    Excitement for Harry Styles' new album Harry's House is bubbling and now the singer has released the full track list for the new record. The grammy-winning artist shared a photo on social media showing the back of the album listing seven songs on Side A and six songs on Side B. It comes soon after the former One Direction star's double weekend stint at Coachella which saw him debut new music including As it Was and the song Boyfriends both of which will feature on Harry's House out in May.

    Sam Smith has released a new song following a two-year hiatus

    After a two year hiatus Sam Smith has made an epic comeback with a powerful ballad. The singer dropped Love Me More and told their fans online that the song was for anyone who feels different and has to stop themselves for being unkind to themselves every day. Sam, who last released music in 2020 with their album Love Goes, also shared an accompanying music video for the track which is out now.

    Lizzo has said she would 'love' to collaborate with K-Pop sensations BTS. The singer was appearing on a recent episode of the Audacy podcast when she admitted that she and BTS member J-Hope were good friend and spending time texting back and forth. Lizzo added she has to be careful what she says due to the group's huge fanbase, but that she would 'asbolutely' work the band in future. We're keeping our fingers crossed! 

    James Corden announced he is leaving the Late Late Show next year

    James Corden has opened up about his decision to step down as host of the next year. The TV star and actor explained to that he's keen to focus on other projects and feels there's more he wants to accomplish in his career. James became emotional as he explained to viewers about his decision to walk away from the show after what will eight and half years. It's been reported that the former Gavin and Stacey star is also considering moving his family back to the UK. 

    And fresh of the back of her surprise Coachella appearance with Billie Eilish, Hayley Williams isn't slowing down as she's set to present a brand new radio show, Everything Is Emo. The Paramore star will be fronting a 20-part series for the BBC as she explores the genre that shaped her career and celebrates her fellow peers from that era. Hayley will also share some personal memories from the band's huge success.
