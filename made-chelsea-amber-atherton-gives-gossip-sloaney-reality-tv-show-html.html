Made in Chelsea: Amber Atherton gives us the gossip on the Sloaney reality TV show

 Posted on June 28, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  813 words  |  Martina Birk

It’s the ‘structured reality’ TV show that picked up where The Only Way Is Essex left off, but added posher accents and a chichi postcode to follow the lives of the privileged Sloane set. Here Made in Chelsea star Amber Atherton, 20, explains to Benji Wilson how much of what we see on screen is true to life


Amber takes a break from business and the box

How did you become involved in Made in Chelsea?

My career has been varied – I’d done some acting [in this year’s Chalet Girls with Ed Westwick and Felicity Jones], been a model [appearing in a fashion shoot for Harper’s Bazaar aged 12] and established an internet jewellery business, myflashtrash.com – but while
I was interning at a fashion marketing company in East London I met one of the producers who asked me if I was interested in her idea for the show.

It’s a ‘structured reality’ show, so are any of your co-stars your real friends?

The producer asked to meet all of my friends, and out of them she cast Rosie [Amber’s confidante on the show, who hopes to work in an art gallery]. Then she asked to meet all of Rosie’s friends, and so on, until she’d chosen all of the characters. The show they wanted to make – Sloanes hanging around bars and shops in Chelsea – isn’t really me, but it’s a fantastic opportunity to expose my [jewellery design] business. Made in Chelsea is being screened in Los Angeles at the moment, and it’s huge.

Do you get along with your new screen friends?

It’s fun – we have such a laugh when we’re filming together.

Have your real pals stood by you?

Friends say I come across well, and my parents are supportive, but some people think it’s cringeworthy. It’s been great business-wise, but I’m not a millionaire yet.

Is anything we see on Made in Chelsea real?

The producers put us in some situations we might not usually be in, and prompt us to talk about things we wouldn’t necessarily discuss. But we never act; our reactions to those situations are real. For instance, they tried to set me up with Hugo [a club promoter] because it would have been a good storyline, but my reaction – saying no, I really don’t want to go out with you – was honest.

And how do you feel about the way you’ve been portrayed?

People have said to me, ‘Oh, you think you’re so much better than everyone else.’ It’s edited so that I do these lingering bitchy stares but I’m more enthusiastic and exuberant in real life; definitely not the ice queen you see on screen. But there’s been a remarkable turnaround: I’ve had lots of emails from young girls asking for business advice, and I’ve even been asked to go back to my old school [Benenden in Kent, alma mater of Princess Anne and Lady Victoria Hervey] to do a talk.

Amber with her Made in Chelsea co-stars

Your real life’s not all posh boys and hanging out in expensive nightclubs, then?

No. I don’t go out much – if I do it’s to a dinner party in Notting Hill, a house party or a fashion event. I tend to hang out in Soho, near to where I live [in her parents’ flat in Covent Garden]. I am so far from a Chelsea socialite. If I ever have money I save it so that I can afford a new function on my website.

We hear you’re a friend of the royals…

The press love playing on the fact that I know Kitty Spencer [Earl Spencer’s daughter and Princess Diana’s niece]. Kitty and I went to prep school together. I’ve seen her a couple of times recently but she lives in South Africa now.

So you’re not really on texting terms with the princes?

Inevitably I see Harry out and about, and I suppose he represents the last chance to bag a prince… But there’s no texting. More people are now pro-ginger because of Harry, though!

What do you think of The Only Way Is Essex?

I haven’t watched it properly, only to see why everyone’s comparing us.  We use a similar setup but it’s a different type of drama really.

Where do you shop?

I grew up in Hong Kong, where my parents still live, so I shop there when I visit. There are lots of cool Korean designers who we don’t get over here. In London, I love the Shop at Bluebird [upmarket boutique on the King’s Road].

You’re single at the moment. What sort of man are you looking for?

He would have to have ambition. I’m turned off by the whole Sloane vibe – if someone comes up to me in head-to-toe Jack Wills? Bye-bye.

Made in Chelsea is on E4 on Mondays at 10pm
