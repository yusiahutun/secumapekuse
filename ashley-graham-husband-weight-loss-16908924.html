Busty bombshell Ashley Graham banishes clothing for raunchy lingerie shoot

 Posted on June 12, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  408 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

The stunning model has no qualms with getting her kit off to flaunt her eye-boggling curves.

And Ashley Graham did just that this week as she stripped down to her undies for a new campaign for the Addition Elle collection, which features underwear and nightwear pieces.

The 31-year-old beauty looked incredible as she worked some angles for the camera in some sizzling lace lingerie.

In the first of the saucy snaps, Ashley can be seen posing on a wicker chair as she models a black lace set with sheer panelling.

Another pic shows the stunner seductively rocking a cheeky cut-out bra and matching undies as she hints at her ample cleavage.

For the final look, the America's Next Top Model judge donned a pretty nude cream set with lace detailing at the waist.

Showcasing her natural beauty for the snaps, Ashley kept her make up minimal.

The bootylicious babe went for a dewy foundation with a swipe of bronze eyeshadow and a thick glossy lip.

She added extra sex appeal to the look by styling her brunette tresses into a sultry bedhead ‘do.

Speaking about her collaboration with the Canadian brand, President of Addition Elle Jonathon Fitzgerald gushed: “Addition Elle is thrilled to continue this collaboration with Ashley.

“Her body confidence message and international recognition are the perfect fit with Addition Elle's messaging of inclusion and confidence.”

Ashley – who is married to husband director and producer, Justin Ervin – has become a passionate advocate of body confidence since her rise to fame in 2016.

Instagram

The stunner has even made history, becoming the first plus size model to grace the over of the Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue.

She’s also fronted issues of Vogue, Elle, Glamour, Harper's Bazaar and walked the catwalk at New York Fashion Week.

Speaking to Page Six about her own battle with self-confidence, the beauty revealed: “It’s the same cycle, it doesn’t matter what generation we are in. Every kid is going to go through the same thing. Having more role models, more women who are like ‘Yeah, I have cellulite. Yeah, it’s even on my arms, not just my legs. My butt is a really bizarre shape but you know what, whatever, I’m just going to go rock it.’

“I think if we had more role models like that, who were really just speaking their truth about their body and the skin that they’re in, then maybe young America would be different.”
